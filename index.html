You're Invited!

Software Freedom Day (SFD) is a worldwide celebration of Free and Open Source Software (FOSS). Our goal in this celebration is to educate the worldwide public about the benefits of using high quality FOSS in education, in government, at home, and in business -- in short, everywhere!

Admission to the event is FREE and open to all enthusiastic people, though we suggest a "Pay-What-You-Can" donation to support FreeGeek Chicago and it's educational and outreach programming. If you write open source software, use free software, or are curious and want to learn about software freedom and the FOSS community, come join us for Software Freedom Day!

About Software Freedom Day

SFD Vision

Our vision is to empower all people to freely connect, create and share in a digital world that is participatory, transparent, and sustainable.

SFD Objectives

  • To celebrate software freedom and the people behind it
  • To foster a general understanding of software freedom, and encourage adoption of free software and open standards
  • To create more equal access to opportunities through the use of participatory technologies
  • To promote constructive dialogue on responsibilities and rights in the information society
  • To be inclusive of organizations and individuals that share the SFD Vision (see above)

What to Bring

Bring yourself, some friends, and an open mind. In addition, you may wish to bring the following items:

  • Laptop - This is not a requisite to participate but we encourage you to bring your own to workshops and the hacking area. We'll provide the WiFi and power.
  • Lunch - FreeGeek will be providing light refreshments throughout the day, including coffee, donuts, chips, soda, water, and other snacks. For a full-on lunch, there are some great neighborhood eats: see Brown-Bag Options below. We'll break at 12:00 noon so you can stuff your face for a bit and socialize as well.
